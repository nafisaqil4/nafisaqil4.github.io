About

Hi! I'm Anupam Chowdhury, a passionate electrical engineer with a deep fascination for the intersection of renewable energy, quantum mechanics, and advanced computational modeling. I graduated with a B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from International Islamic University Chittagong (IIUC) with a CGPA of 3.63/4.00, where I developed a strong foundation in both theoretical and applied engineering disciplines.

Research Interests

My research interests span across multiple cutting-edge domains:

  • Smart Grid Technologies: Developing resilient microgrid frameworks for sustainable energy distribution
  • Computational Electromagnetics: Advanced modeling and simulation of electromagnetic phenomena
  • Quantum Computing & Materials Science: Exploring quantum mechanics applications in next-generation technologies
  • Renewable Energy Systems: Photovoltaic systems, battery storage, and energy optimization

Throughout my academic journey, I've gained extensive hands-on experience with industry-standard simulation tools including COMSOL Multiphysics, Quantum Espresso, MATLAB/Simulink, and Cadence Virtuoso. My undergraduate thesis, "A Micro-grid based Operational Framework for Grid Resiliency Enhancement in Bangladesh," involved sophisticated modeling of PV systems, bidirectional battery energy storage systems (BESS), and advanced inverter control using dq-transformation techniques. This work demonstrated significant improvements in grid resilience under various fault conditions.

Currently, I'm expanding my expertise as a Research Assistant at Mahdy's Research Academy, where I'm exploring wave-based quantum mechanics and density functional theory (DFT). This experience is sharpening my skills in computational modeling and deepening my understanding of quantum phenomena at the atomic scale.

Future Aspirations

I'm eager to pursue graduate studies in electrical engineering or applied physics, with the goal of contributing to groundbreaking research in renewable energy integration, quantum computing applications, and advanced materials. My ultimate vision is to bridge the gap between theoretical innovation and practical implementation, creating technologies that address real-world challenges in energy sustainability and computational advancement.

I believe that the future of engineering lies at the intersection of multiple disciplines, and I'm committed to continuous learning and exploration. Whether it's developing more efficient energy systems, exploring quantum phenomena, or creating innovative solutions through computational modeling, I'm driven by curiosity and a passion for making a meaningful impact through science and engineering.
