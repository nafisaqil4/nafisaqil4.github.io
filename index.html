About Me

I am a recent graduate with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the International Islamic University Chittagong (IIUC), achieving a CGPA of 3.63/4.00.

My academic journey has been driven by a passion for enhancing grid resiliency and exploring sustainable energy solutions. My undergraduate thesis focused on developing a micro-grid based operational framework to improve grid stability in Bangladesh, where I extensively modeled and simulated PV systems, battery storage, and inverter controls using MATLAB.

Beyond my core studies in power systems, I have actively pursued interests in quantum mechanics, gaining hands-on experience with simulation tools like COMSOL and Quantum Espresso. I am eager to apply my analytical skills and research experience to solve complex challenges in the energy sector.

Research Experience

Thesis: A Micro-grid Framework for Grid Resiliency

Keywords: Micro-grid, Islanding, BESS, Resilience, Framework

Investigated micro-grid frameworks to improve Bangladesh's grid resilience and minimize reliance on fossil fuels. My work involved modeling and simulating PV systems, MPPT, bidirectional battery storage, and advanced inverter control using dq-transformation in MATLAB. A key finding was the significant improvement in the resilience index from 0.291 (conventional grid) to 0.657 (micro-grid) under three-phase fault conditions.
